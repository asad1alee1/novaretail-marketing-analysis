📊 Dataset: 457 customers (merged RFM + marketing profiles) 🗓 Analysis Date: March 2026 📦 Orders dataset: 4,378 transactions
Executive Summary OVERVIEW

NovaRetail's 457 active customers were segmented into four distinct behavioural clusters using K-means clustering on recency, frequency, monetary value, email engagement, website visits, and discount usage. The analysis reveals a clear hierarchy of customer value and uncovers three critical strategic insights: (1) email engagement is a poor predictor of long-term customer value, (2) discount-heavy customers represent a margin risk rather than a revenue opportunity, and (3) paid-search and organic-search acquisition channels deliver significantly higher lifetime value than the dominant email-acquisition channel.

T1. Data Exploration TECHNICAL
Dataset Overview & Missing Values

Three files were merged on client_id. The RFM file contained 457 complete records (no missing values). The marketing profile file (560 rows) had 39 missing observations in age, income, website_visits, and email_opens — all below 7% threshold and imputed with column medians for clustering. 103 customers appeared in the marketing file but lacked RFM data and were excluded from clustering.

Missing value strategy: Median imputation was applied to age (7%), income (7%), website_visits (7%) and email_opens (7%) prior to clustering. All imputed values fell within reasonable ranges and did not distort cluster centroids materially.

Key Exploration Findings
📧

Email engagement ≠ Customer value (r = 0.03–0.09)

Email opens and clicks have near-zero correlation with CLV (r=0.032 and r=0.095 respectively). High email activity does not translate to higher spending or lifetime value. Cluster 1 has the highest email engagement (avg 19 opens) yet lower CLV than Cluster 2 whose members rarely open emails.

🏷️

Discount users have higher CLV but lower transaction value

Customers who used discounts averaged CLV of €2,463 vs €2,078 for non-discount users — seemingly positive. However, their average spend per order (€919) is lower than non-discount users (€959), indicating discounts are inflating purchase counts while compressing margin. Cluster 0 — where 100% of customers used discounts — has the lowest CLV of all segments at €663.

🔍

Acquisition channel dramatically predicts CLV

Paid search customers average CLV of €3,708 and organic search €2,991, while email-acquired customers (49% of the base) average only €1,632 — less than half the value of paid search. This represents a major resource-allocation problem: nearly half the customer base comes from the channel with the worst long-term value.

🗺️

Regional discount variation is significant

East (48.6%) and South (49.1%) have nearly double the discount usage of the North region (23.1%). This suggests regionally differentiated pricing behaviour or sales team practices that deserve separate treatment.

T2. Customer Segmentation CLUSTERING
Methodology

K-means clustering was applied using six features: recency, frequency, monetary value, website visits, email opens, and discount usage. Features were standardised (z-score) before clustering. The optimal number of clusters was selected by comparing silhouette scores for k=2 through k=7.

T3. Marketing Effectiveness ANALYSIS
1. Email Engagement vs Customer Value

Avg CLV by Email Opens Quintile

Email Opens vs Clicks Correlation (r = 0.90)

Email opens and email clicks are highly correlated with each other (r=0.90) but almost completely uncorrelated with CLV or monetary value (r≈0.03). This means the email campaigns are attracting and retaining customers who click a lot but do not spend. The most valuable customers (Champions) average only 6 email opens — they buy without being nudged by email.

Conclusion: NovaRetail is over-investing in broad email marketing. Email volume predicts click behaviour but does NOT predict revenue or lifetime value. Reducing email campaign frequency to inactive/low-value segments will cut costs without hurting revenue.

3. Acquisition Channel Value Comparison

Avg CLV by Acquisition Channel (€)

Share of Customer Base by Channel

Paid search (€3,708) and organic search (€2,991) customers generate the highest lifetime value, yet together they represent only 23% of the customer base. Email-acquired customers (49% of base) generate the lowest CLV at €1,632 — less than half that of paid search. This is a structural mismatch between acquisition spend and value generation.

Key insight: Every euro spent acquiring a customer via paid search generates approximately 2.3× the lifetime return of an email-acquired customer. NovaRetail should explore reallocating part of its email campaign budget toward paid search and organic content investment.

4. Strategic Recommendations BUSINESS REPORT

The following three actions are prioritised by expected ROI, feasibility for a medium-sized retailer, and alignment with a constrained marketing budget. All are grounded directly in the data findings above.

Action 01 · Highest Priority

🎯 Launch a VIP Retention Programme for Champions

Champions (Cluster 2, 80 customers) generate an average CLV of €8,280 — nearly 4× the overall average. Their average recency is only 17 days, meaning they are active right now. Yet they receive the same generic email campaigns as everyone else, despite being virtually unresponsive to email (avg 6 opens).

Replace bulk email with a lightweight VIP programme: priority customer service access, early product previews, and non-discount loyalty rewards (points, exclusive products). This costs significantly less than discount promotions while signalling genuine recognition. Given an assumed gross margin of 35–40% (typical for lifestyle retail), the incremental retention of even 10 additional Champions represents ~€80,000+ in lifetime revenue.

Action 02 · High Priority

✂️ Discontinue Blanket Discounting — Restrict to Upsell Use Only

Cluster 0 (121 customers, 26.5% of the base) is defined entirely by discount dependency — every single customer used a discount, yet they have the lowest CLV in the dataset (€663). East and South regions, where discount rates reach 49%, are generating systematically lower-margin customers. Continued blanket discounting in these regions is training customers to wait for promotions rather than buying at full price.

NovaRetail should eliminate untargeted discount codes from mass email campaigns (e.g., the February 2017 SAVE10 campaign style). Instead, restrict discounts to two specific uses: (1) first-time purchase conversion for new high-potential prospects, and (2) strategic upsell to move Engaged Potentials toward Champion-tier spend. At an assumed 10% discount on €656 average spend per Cluster 0 customer, removing the discount saves approximately €7,938 per campaign cycle from this segment alone — without necessarily losing sales from customers who were purchasing due to the product, not the price.

Action 03 · Medium Priority

📡 Rebalance Acquisition Budget Away from Email, Toward Paid & Organic Search

Email acquisition accounts for 49% of the customer base but produces the lowest CLV (€1,632). Paid search customers (11% of base) generate CLV of €3,708 — 127% higher. Organic search (13% of base) generates €2,991. The current acquisition mix is heavily tilted toward a channel that systematically under-delivers on lifetime value.

We recommend redirecting 20–30% of the email campaign budget toward: (a) SEO content investment to grow the organic search channel (currently only 59 customers but CLV of €2,991), and (b) a measured test of paid search scaling to validate whether the CLV advantage holds as volume grows. Simultaneously, email campaigns should be reduced to 2–3 targeted segments (Champions, Engaged Potentials, and new prospects) rather than the full 560-customer base — cutting software and management costs while maintaining relevance.

Methodology Notes TECHNICAL

K-means clustering was performed on standardised features (StandardScaler). Silhouette analysis across k=2–7 selected k=4 as optimal. Clustering features: recency, frequency, monetary, website_visits, email_opens, discount_used. Missing values imputed by column median. Correlation analysis used Pearson r. Profit margin estimates use a 35–40% assumption typical for lifestyle retail; actual margin data would sharpen financial projections. All monetary figures in Euros (€).
